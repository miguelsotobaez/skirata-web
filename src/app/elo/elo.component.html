It is an ELO based scoring system.

Every duel you do, saber or force, goes through an algorithm where your and rival skills is measured, finally the result is your ELO score.

Every player that starts playing begin with a base ELO of 1200, after the duel 10 this base ELO will start working.

10 duels in per category are required to begin qualifying.

No, an account is not necessary, the system is based on the nick of the player. The colors of your nickname can vary and it will be taken as a player.

Example:

^ 2M ^ 7i ^ 1Nick

^ 3M ^ 7i ^ 4Nick

This nick will be the same player.

It would be different if:

^ 2M ^ 7i ^ 1Nick

^ 3M ^ 7i ^ 0NICK

In this second case they are different players, since it differs from upper or lower case.

Our Jedi Knight community is already accustomed to playing without registration, the times it has been implemented almost nobody registers their account.

The site has an automatic detection system that runs every night, there are inconsistencies such as nick encroachment, nick changes in duels and other things. When a player is detected committing this fault, the ELO score of all his nicknames are penalized and banned from the server for 1 month. Subsequently, the ELO of the injured player is restored.

When a player is in a certain ELO rank they will be classified in a category, then the rank and categories:

0 - 1099 = Iron

1100 - 1199 = Bronze

1200 - 1299 = Silver

1300 - 1399 = Gold

1400 - 1499 = Platinum

1500 - 1599 = Diamond

1600+ = Immortal

Yes, for people who stop playing, they will be penalized according to their category

If the player is Iron and inactive for 200 days, 10 ELO will be deducted.

If the player is Bronze and inactive for 120 days, 10 ELO will be deducted.

If the player is Silver and inactive for 60 days, 10 ELO will be deducted.

If the player is Gold and inactive for 30 days, 10 ELO will be deducted.

If the player is Platinum and inactive for 22 days, 10 ELO will be deducted.

If the player is Diamond and inactive for 18 days, 10 will be deducted from ELO.

If the player is Immortal and inactive for 10 days, 10 will be deducted from ELO.
